## 2. Single most promising construction for the product's "semantic layer, no GPU"

**PPMI-factorization (PMI-SVD / spectral-Laplacian on the PPMI term-graph), RRF-fused, gated to mismatch corpora only.** These are the same object — Levy-Goldberg's word2vec-as-SVD and the diffusion-map eigenbasis both factorize the positive-PMI term-term matrix. They give the only repeatable positive delta (nfcorpus +0.006 to +0.011), they're glass-box (you can print the topic/eigen-axis terms — NMF topic#3 = `[stem, cells, differentiation, embryonic, pluripotent, ipscs]`), they build in 14–37s CPU, and the lift is structurally explainable (it bridges `phytochemicals↔mechanisms` type vocabulary gaps BM25 can't).

The honest framing for a pitch: **this is not a dense replacement, it's a cheap mismatch-corpus booster.** It earns its place as an RRF term that you *gate on* when a corpus shows vocabulary mismatch (the diagnostic battery already detects this), and gate *off* on aligned corpora where it's pure drag.

## 3. The honest reality: why they're all weak, and which would scale

**They are all weak for one reason, and it is a data reason, not a math reason:** scifact (5,183 docs) and nfcorpus (3,633 docs) are too small to estimate a stable latent geometry. Co-occurrence counts are too sparse — the spectral eigenvalues all bunch in 0.46–0.86 (no clean low-frequency structure), TruncatedSVD captures only 21–34% variance at k=300, NMF cosine collapses to 0.09/0.14 because topics can't rank *within* a topic, and 3-way tensor reconstruction tops out at 0.30 correlation. The k-sweeps are monotone with **no plateau** (LSA scifact 0.235@k50 → 0.558@k700) — that's not finding semantics, it's just reconstructing the sparse matrix as rank → full. This matches the standing memory note exactly: raw co-occurrence is sparse on tiny corpora.

**What would scale with more docs:** PMI-SVD and spectral-Laplacian. Both are the proven LSA/GloVe/word2vec family — on Wikipedia-scale or full-8.8M-MARCO co-occurrence, the PMI matrix is dense and well-conditioned, and these *are* how the field built semantic vectors before GPUs. The monotone-no-plateau sweeps are the signature of a method starved of data, not a method that's fundamentally capped. **What will NOT scale:** random VSA (fixed-random codebook carries zero semantics at any corpus size — the dimension sweep is flat by construction), pure JL (a lossy compressor, can't exceed its source at any k), 3-way tensor (order-2 is denser and better-conditioned — going to order-3 always adds noise; this is why the whole field lives at order 2), and lattice-coord features (the 3D-plane places by value not meaning — 0.9995 mean pairwise cosine, a coordinate system not a vector space; this confirms the standing finding and is a genuine dead end for semantics).

## 4. The ONE next build

**Build the PPMI-SVD semantic vector on the full 8.8M-doc MARCO co-occurrence matrix (not on a 5k BEIR toy), and measure whether the mismatch-lift survives at scale.** Concretely: stream the corpus once to a sparse VxV shifted-PPMI term-term matrix (neg=5, window=10 — the tuned winner), `scipy.sparse.linalg.svds` to k=300, IDF-pool to doc vectors, RRF-fuse with the existing lexical lattice, and measure nDCG@10 on MARCO dev-small **gated** (fuse only when a per-query mismatch signal fires). This is the single experiment that distinguishes "co-occurrence factorization is fundamentally weak" from "it was just data-starved on BEIR-tiny." Every lens here points to it: PPMI is the only thing that ever helped, and tiny-corpus sparsity is the only thing that held it back. If the lift grows from +0.01 to something material at 8.8M docs, that is the no-GPU semantic layer; if it stays at +0.01, the honest product is lexical-lattice + supervised qrels bridges, and the "free vectors from geometry" thesis is closed.
